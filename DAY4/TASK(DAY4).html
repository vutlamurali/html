RESTAURNT MENU ITEM LIST

In a restaurant, the menu is a list of food and beverages offered to customers and the prices. A menu may be à la carte – which presents a list of options from which customers choose – or table d'hôte, in which case a pre-established sequence of courses is offered. Menus may be printed on paper sheets provided to the diners, put on a large poster or display board inside the establishment, displayed outside the restaurant, or put on a digital screen. Since the late 1990s, some restaurants have put their menus online. Menus are also often a feature of very formal meals other than in restaurants, for example at weddings. In the 19th and 20th centuries printed menus were often used for society dinner-parties in homes; indeed this was their original use in Europe.

A restaurant business plan's executive summary is a brief overview of all the information in the plan. It's the first section that potential investors will read, so a strong executive summary is important for starting a business and getting funding

    executive summary should include information about the restaurant's:

  • Financial plans: How much funding is needed, loans and lines of credit, and how long it will take to become profitable
  • Ownership structure
  • Location
  • Type of restaurant
  • Customer experience
  • Success factors
  • Risks of the business mode
